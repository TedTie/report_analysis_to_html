# Pitfalls

- Do not hard-code conclusions from one project as general rules.
- Do not assume a fixed report structure.
- Do not assume fixed data dimensions.
- Do not assume channels must always be separated.
- Do not assume every person must always be displayed individually.
- Do not assume specific business terms should always be replaced.
- Do not write internal notes into the formal report.
- Do not put production guidance beside titles or subtitles.
- Do not generate final content before clarifying ambiguous terms that affect meaning.
- Do not generate HTML before the report document is confirmed.
- Do not treat one visual style as the universal template.
- Do not trade readability for decoration.
- Do not compress insights until the original meaning changes.
- Do not add claims that the user did not provide or confirm.
- Do not package HTML inside nested folders when the user needs a shareable file.
- Do not silently continue without image generation when the design requires generated visual assets.
